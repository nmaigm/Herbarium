Avena fatua L.
Nyman, Consp. Fl. Europ.29 (1878).

Synonyms:
Cakile aegyptiaca Willd., Sp. Pl., ed. 4, 3:417 (1800).
Cakile hispanica Jord., Diagn. Esp. Nouv. 345 (1864).
Cakile littoralis Jord., Diagn. Esp. Nouv. 345 (1864).

Morphology:
Annual, succulent herb, up to 60 cm. long and glabrous. Stem decumbent, terete, solid and branched. Internodes 2 – 4 cm. x o.2 - 0.4 cm. Leaves up to 6 cm. long. Lower leaves oblong-ovate in outline, petiolate. Petiole glabrous and up to 2.5 cm. long. Blade 2 – 3.5 cm. x 1 – 1.5 cm, simple with entire to sinuate-dentate margin and acute apex. The upper leaves simple and petiolate. Petiole up to 1.5 cm. long. Blade 1.5 – 2.5 cm. x 0.5 - 1 cm., oblong ovate, dentate with acute apex. Inflorescence raceme. Flowers 3 - 7 mm. long and pedicellate. Pedicels glabrous, 2 – 4 mm. long and thick. Sepals glabrous, green- yellow, 2.5 - 4 mm. x1- 1.5 mm. ovate oblong in outline with narrow membranous margin. Petals violet-lilac 4 – 6.5 mm. x 1 – 2.5 mm. clawed, limb obovate with obtuse apex. Stamen 3 – 5 mm. long, with glabrous, filament and ovate anthers. Ovary smooth with inconspicuous style and flattened stigma. Siliqua 1.5 – 2 cm. x 0.4 – 0.6 cm., ribbed, glabrous, indehiscent with 2- segmented. The upper segment longer than the lower with pyramidal shaped and one seeded. The lower segment short, cylindrical with two prominent lateral projections basally and one seeded. Beak 2- 3 mm long and seedless. Seeds D-shaped, 2.5– 3.5mm. x 1 - 2mm. brown with sub-terminal hilum and smooth surface.
