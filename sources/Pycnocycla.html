Pycnocycla tomentosa Decne

Morphology:
Perennial bright green and erect herb, up to 80 cm long, with many basal branches. Branches, 25 - 30 cm long with terete and long internodes, each to 20 cm long. Leaves alternate, with basal sheath and ovate in outline. Lower leaves, petiolate and reach to 18 cm long. Sheath, broad ovate; 0.3 -0.5 x 0.2 - 0.4 cm with broad membranous margins. Petiole glabrous and up to 14 cm long. Blade usually 2 - 3 pinnatisect with lanceolate lobes, 0.8 - 3.5 x 1.0 -1.5 cm and pubescent. The upper leaves are reduced to sheaths, o.3 - 0.4 x 0.2 - 0.3 cm.

Flowers in simple umbel inflorescence. Umbel solitary on a long peduncle; 3.0 - 5.0 cm long, umbel rays numerous and densely wooly. It is pedicelate, bracteate and bracteolate, pentamerous, tetracyclic, and bisexual surrounded by male (unisexual) flower, syncarpous and actinomorphic. Bract simple, linear, acuminate, and pubescent, 4.0 - 5.0 x 1.0 - 1.5 mm, while bracteoles 3.0 - 4.0 x 1.0 mm, linear-lanceolate, acuminate and pubescent. Calyx of 5 persistent sepals. Sepals 1.0 - 2.0 x 0.5 – 1.0 mm long ovate-triangular, acute and pubescent. Petals white, bilobed unequal, oblong with emarginated inflexed apex and pubescent. Stamens five with long filament 1.0-1.5 mm and dorsifixed, equal anther lobes. Ovary, elliptic ± cylindrical, densely covered by hairs with 2 locule. Style very short, 1.0 mm long and erect. Stylopodium shorter than style, 0.5 mm and conical.

Fruit, cylindrical to ellipsoid, 0.8 -1.2cm x 0.1 cm, pubescent, crowned with the persistent calyx-teeth.
