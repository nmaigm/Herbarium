Sida alba L.
Sp. Pl., ed. 2, 960 (1763).

Synonyms:
Sida spinosa L., Sp. Pl., ed. 1, 683 (1753), Pro Parte.

Morphology:
Stellate-pubescent annual or short-lived perennial, 30-80 cm; stems erect, branched; leaf blade 1-4 x 0.3-2.5 cm, ovate or ovate-lanceolate to elliptic, margin serrate-dentate, the base rounded, the apex acute or obtuse; petiole 0.3-2 cm, with 1-3 spiny tubercles on the stem at the base; stipules 2-5 mm, filiform; flowers axillary, solitary or 2-5 in fascicles pedicel 2-5 mm, elongate in fruit to 2 cm, jointed in the upper part; sepals 3-4 mm. slightly accrescent in fruit, the lobes triangular, acute; petals 4-5 mm, white or cream: fruit depressed-globose; mericarps 4-5, 2-3 mm, trigonous, with 2 apical divergent awns 1 mm; seeds 1.5 mm, glabrous, brownish-black.
