Bassia indica (Wight) A. J. Scott
Feddes Repert, 89: 108 (1978).

Synonyms:
Kochia indica Wight, Icon. PL. Ind. Orient., t. 1791 (1852).
Bassia joppensis Bornm. & Dinsm., Repert. Spec. Reg. Veg. 17: 274 (1921).
Kochia scoparia (L.) Schrad, subsp. indica (Wight) Aellen, Mitt. Basler Bot. Ges. 2: 15 (1954).

Morphology:
Annual herb 0.5-1.8 m. pilose-pubescent; stems erect, richly branched, the branches spreading or ascending: leaves 0.5-2.5 x 0.2-0.6 cm, oblong-elliptic to linear-lanceolate, softly hairy especially on the lower surface: upper leaves small, densely white-hairy: perianth segments 5. white-hairy.connate for most of their length, with 1 mm spreading flowers in small clusters forming leafy spike: stamens 5. exerted, anthers yellow: wings in fruit.
