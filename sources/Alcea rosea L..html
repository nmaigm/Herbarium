Alcea rosea L.
Sp. Pl., ed. 1, 687 (1753).

Synonyms:
Alcea ficifolia L., Sp. Pl., ed. 1, 687 (1753).
Alcea rosea (L.) Cav., Diss. 91 (1786).
Althaea ficifolia (L.) Cav., Diss. 92 (1786).

Morphology:
Perennial or short-lived perennial, 0.8-1.5 m; stems erect, unbranched or little branched, rough, with stellate-hairs; leaf blade 4-8 cm, rounded or ovate, 3-7-lobed, crenate- dentate, stellate-hairy on both surfaces; petiole 5-15 cm; flowers 5-8 cm diam., axillary, solitary or 2-3 in fascicles, on long spicate racemes; pedicel 0.5-1.5 cm, elongate in fruit; epicalyx segments 6-7, 1.2-1.6 cm, the lobes ovate-lanceolate, stellate-hairy; calyx to 2 cm, stellate-hairy; corolla 5-7.5 cm; mericarps deeply grooved dorsally; margins 25-40, with 2 parallel wings.
