Xanthium strumarium L.
Sp. Pl., ed. 1, 987 (1753).

Synonyms:
Xanthium indicum König ex Roxb., Hort. Beng. 67 (1814).
Xanthium italicum Moretti in Brugnat., Giorn. Fis., ser. 2, 5: 326 (1822).
Xanthium brasilicum Vellozo, Fl. Flum. 399 (1825).
Xanthium cavanillesii Schouw, Ann. Sci. Nat.. sér. 3, 12: 357 (1849).
Xanthium strumarium L. subsp. cavanillesi (Schouw) D. Löve & Dansereau, Canad. J. Bot. 37: 205 (1959).
Xanthium strumarium L. subsp. italium (Moretti) D. Löve, Bot. J. Linn. Soc. 71: 271 (1976).

Morphology:
Scabrid-tomentose annual, 30-80 cm, with appressed minute short white hairs: stems branched, tomentose, later glabrescent, unarmed; leaves 4-8(-12) x 3-6(-8) cm, deltate, irregularly lobed, scabrid, the margins dentate-crenate, the base cordate or cuneate: petiole 3-8 cm, pubescent; male capitula in axillary and terminal racemes, pedunculate; fruiting involucre 1-1.2 x 0.8-1 cm, subglobose, tomentose-hispid, armed with hooked spines; beak 2-3 mm, of 2 slightly incurved pubescent rigid spines.
