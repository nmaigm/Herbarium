Eryngium campestre L.
Sp.PI., ed. I, 233(1753).

Morphology:
Perennial erect green, glabrous herb up to 60 cm. long. Stem simple, dichotomously branched from above with angular and striate internodes. Internode reach to 3.0 cm. long. Leaves alternate, 3-pinnatisect, triangular-ovate in outline, mostly ternate with 2-pinnatifid segment. Lower leaf reach to 17.0 cm. long with long petiole reach to 6.0 cm. long and short inconspicuous sheath. Sheath triangular-ovate in outline with narrow membranous margins, 1.0-2.0 x 0.1-0.3 cm. Blade reach to 9.0 cm. long. Upper leaves are sessile and small, ovate in outline 2-4 pinnatipartite with linear-lanceolate segment. Segment with spiny margin. Flowers in compound umbels inflorescence. The umbel, head-like, surrounded by an involucre of several large, mostly spiny, bracts. Bract and bracteoles present. Flowers hermaphrodite, sessile and perfect. Bracts 5-7, linear-lanceolate, simple or with 2 - 4 lateral spines 2.0-5.0 cm. long. Bracteoles 1.5-3.0 cm; entire, lanceolate, spiny and longer than sepals. Calyx with 5 conspicuous sepals. Sepal ovate-lanceolate, 2.5 - 3.0 x 1.0 mm aristate and longer than petals. Corolla with 5 white or creamy petals. Petal 2.5 x 2.0 mm; obovate with inflexed emarginated apex. Androecium with 5 stamens. Stamen with short filament and equal dorsifixed anther lobes. Gynoecium, subinferior with two carpels. Ovary with two locule, syncarpous, style filiform while stylopodium dilated. Fruit, obovoid-subglobose, covered with lanceolate scales, carpophore adnate to mericarps, mericarps with obsolete ribs.
