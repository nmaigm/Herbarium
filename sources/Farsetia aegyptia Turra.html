Farsetia aegyptia Turra subsp. aegyptia.
Farsetia Nov. Gen. 5, t. 1 (1765).

Synonyms:
Farsetia oblongata C. Presl, Abh. KÖnigl. BÖhm. Ges. Wiss., ser. 5, 3:438 (1845).
Farsetia ovalis Boiss., Diagn. Pl. Orient. 8: 32 (1849).

Morphology:
Annual or short-lived perennial shrub, up to 60 cm. long, covered with bi-armed hairs. Stem erect, terete, solid and ascending branched. Internodes 1 – 2.2 cm. x o.1 - 0.2 cm. Leaves simple, sessile, 1 – 2.5 cm. long x 0.1 – 0.5 cm., oblong ovate, obtuse and entire margin. Inflorescence racemes. Flowers 8 - 15 mm. long and pedicellate. Pedicels hairy 4 - 6 mm. long. Sepals yellowish green, hairy, 8 -10 mm. x 2 mm., linear-ovate in outline with narrow membranous margin. Petals pab brown-yellow , 9 -15 mm. x 2 – 3 mm., clawed, limb linear to obovate with obtuse apex. Stamen 8 – 9 mm. long, with glabrous filament and oblong anthers. Ovary hairy with pubescent, style, 2.5 – 3 mm. long and long capitate stigma. Silicula 1 – 1.9 cm. x 0.2- 0.8 cm., ± elliptic to sub-orbicular rounded at the ends, flat with one veined septum, dehiscent, hairy with 1.5 – 2 mm. long beak. Septum membranous and seeds in one row per locule. Seeds 3 – 4 mm. x 4- 5 mm., compressed with broad white marginal wings, sub-globose with terminal hilum, smooth surface and brown-orange colour.
