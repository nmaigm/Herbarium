Pimpinella etabica Schweinf
Verh. Zool.- Bot. Vereins. Wien 18:667(1868).

Morphology:
Annual yellowish green glabrous herb, up to 25 cm long. stem erect with springly branched above. Internode 1.5 - 4.0 cm., terete with finely striate. Leaves alternate, pinnatisect and ovate in outline. Lower leaves trisect, long, 4.0-8.0 cm. long, petiolate with conspicuous basal sheaths. Sheath long triangular – ovate 0.5 -1.4 X 0.2 - 0.4 cm with narrow membranous margins and hairy. Petiole long 2.0 – 4.0 cm length. Blade trisect, sub orbicular to ovate in outline with dentate segments. Upper leaves sessile, ternate with linear – acute segments. Flowers in compound umbels inflorescence. Umbels terminal on short peduncle 1.5-2.5 cm. long, many rayed; 5 - 9 rays. Flower pedicelate, bisexual and perfect. Bract absent while bracteole present few, triangular to long ovate, acute and hairy. 1.1 – 2.2 X 0.1 - 0.3 mm. Calyx with five-minute sepals. Corolla white with five petals. Petal obovate notched with inflexed apex, uninerved, 0.5 - 1.0 X 0.5 mm. Androecium five stamen, stamen with long filament 1.0 -1.5 mm and equal dorsifixed anther lobes. Ovary sub-inferior with deflexed long style and conical stylopodium. Fruit ovoid – globose, 1.2 – 2.0 mm. laterally depressed, hairy and greenish. Carpophore bipartite, slender, mericarps with densely white hair.
