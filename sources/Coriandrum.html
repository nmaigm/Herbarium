Coriandrum sativum L.
SP. PI., ed. I, 256 (1753).

Morphology:
Annual aromatic glabrous herb up to 80 cm. tall. Stem erect with many basal dichotomous branches. Internode terete, striated, 1.8 - 3.2 cm. Leaves alternate, pinnatisect, reach to 11.0 cm. long. Lower leaves long petiolated, up to 10.8 cm. Sheath ovate-elliptic with broad membranous margins, 5.0-7.0 x 2.0 - 3.0 mm. Petiole is of 6.8- 7.6 cm. Blade ovate-oblong in outline, 2-3 lobed or ternatisect with ovate-cuneate lobes, 4.5 - 5.0 x 2.3 - 2.5 cm. Upper leaves are sessile, dissected into ovate-linear lobes, 2.5-8.0 cm with sheath each to 0.5–1.5 cm x 0.3-0.5 cm. Blade 2.0- 6.5 x 2.5- 3.0 cm. Flowers in compound terminal umbels and opposite the leaves, umbel-rays 3-7, rays unequal, 1.0-3.0 cm. long. Bract absent while bracteoles linear-lanceolate and acuminate to 1.0 -2.0 mm long. Flowers pedicellate, bisexual and staminate in each umbel. Calyx with five unequal persistent sepals. Sepal ovate-deltoid and acuminate to 1.0 - 2.0 mm. Corolla white, petals of outer flowers radiant, deeply equal or unequal bilobed, broad obovate to elliptic with inflexed apex and many nerved while the petals of inner flowers are uninerved, obovate with smaller incurved emarginate apex, 1.0-1.5 mm. Androecium with 5 yellow stamens. Stamen with long filament 0.5-1.5 mm long and equal, dorsifixed anther lobes. Ovary globose to 3.0 - 4.0 mm. Styles filiform and longer than the conical stylopodium. Fruit globose, 3.5 – 5.0 mm hardly compressed, glabrous, greenish-brown and crowned with the persistent calyx- teeth. Mericarps with 5, undulate primary and 4 prominent secondary ribs.
