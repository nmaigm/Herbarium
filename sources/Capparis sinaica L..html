Capparis sinaica Veill. in Duh.
Traité Arbr. Arbust., ed. 2, 1: 144 (1801).

Synonyms:
Capparis cartilaginea Decne., Ann. Sci. Nat. Bot., ser. 2, 3: 273 (1835).
Capparis galeata Fresen., Mus. Senkenb. 2: 111 (1837).
Capparis spinosaL. subsp. cartilaginea (Decne.) Maire & Weiller in Maire, Fl. Afr. Nord 12: 120 (1965).

Morphology:
Shrub 1-2.5 m; stems many, intricately branched; leaves 2.5-4.5 x 1-3.5 cm, long-petiolate, thick, ovate-oblong, with a spiny tip: buds 1.5 x 2 cm; flowers 7-8 cm diam., solitary, axillary, zygomorphic, white; sepals tip; stipular spines short, recurved, flower unequal; the posterior 4 cm, largest, with a deep cavity; petals unequal, the anterior pair free, the posterior coherent; ovary borne on a long gynophore; fruit 6-8 cm. pyriform berry, on a stipe 2.5-4 cm; seeds numerous, reniform
