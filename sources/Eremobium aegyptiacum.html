Eremobium aegyptiacum (Spreng.)
Asch.& Schweinf. ex Boiss., FI. Orient. Suppl. 30 (1888).

Synonyms:
Malcolmia aegyptiaca Spreng., Syst. Veg. 2:898 (1825).

Morphology:
Annual or biennial herb, up to 40 cm. long, prostrate to ascending, canescent with scattered stellate appressed hairs. Stem erect, terete, solid and branched. Internodes 1 - 3.5 cm. long x o.1 - 0.2 cm. width. Leaves simple, sessile, 0.9 – 2 cm. long x 0.2 – 0.5 cm. width, elliptic to oblong, entire and obtuse. Inflorescence are racemes. Flowers 5 - 9 mm. long and pedicellate. Pedicels hairy 2 - 4 mm. long. Sepals greenish, hairy, 5 -9 mm. x 0.5 – o.1 mm., oblong-linear in outline with narrow membranous margin. Petals yellowish, 5 -9 mm. x 1 –2 mm., clawed, limb ±linear, entire with acute apex. Stamen, 4 – 7 mm. long with glabrous, broad filament and oblong-linear anthers. Ovary hairy with glabrous, short style, 2-3 mm. long and capitate stigma. Siliqua 1.3 – 3 cm. x o.1- o.2 cm., cylindrical, torulose, pubescent and dehiscent. Seeds in one row per locule. Beak short 1mm. long. Seeds 2 – 2.5 mm. x 1-1.2 mm., oblong, ± marginate-winged with sub-terminal hilum, glandular surface and yellow or greenish yellow colour.
