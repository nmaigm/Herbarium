Eryngium creticum Lam.
Encycl. 4:754 (1798).

Morphology:
The morphological characters are similar to those in E. campestre differ only with regard to:
1- Plant glaucous 20 - 50 cm. long
2- Stems, many, branched below.
3- Basal leaves shorter, 5.0 - 8.0 cm. long.
4- Petiole longer than the blade.
5- Upper leaves palmatisect.
6- Inflorescence metallic blue at maturity.
7- Bract linear-subulate, mostly 5.
8- Bracteoles 3-cuspidate lobes.
9- Calyx teeth shorter, ovate-mucronate with scarious margin, 2.0 - 2.5 mm. long.
10- Fruit scaly bristly, ribbed.
