Abelmoschus esculentus (L.) Moench.
Meth. Pl. Horti. Bot. 617 (1794)

Synonyms:
Hibiscus esculentus L., Sp. Pl., ed. 1, 696 (1753).

Morphology:
Annual or perennial herbs. Leaves stipulate, simple, palmately veined. Flowers solitary axillary or in racemes. Epicalyx of many episepals. Staminal tube divided below the tip into numerous filaments, bearing anthers to the base. Style with 5 cushion-like stigmas. Fruit a capsule, several times longer than calyx, dehiscing by 5 valves.
