Pseudorlaya pumila (L.) Grande
Nuov. Giorn. Bot. Ital., n, s., 32: 86(1925).

Synonyms:
Caucalis pumila L., syst. Nat., ed. 10,955 (1759).
Caucalis maritima Gouan, Hort. Reg. Monsp. 135 (1762).
Orlaya maritima (Gouan) Koch, Nov. Act. Nat. Cur. 12, 1:79(1824).
Orlaya pumila (L.) Halacsy, Consp. FI. Graec. 1:622(1901).

Morphology:
Annual, erect, pubescent herb, 10 - 25 cm. tall, branched from the base, with distinct ribbed internodes, which rich to 1.9 cm. long. Leaves, alternate, petiolate, hairy, oblong-ovate in outline, 2 - 4 pinnatisect and reach to 9.0 cm. long. Sheath triangular-long ovate; 0.5 - 1.2 x 0.2 - 0.4 cm. with membranous hairy margin. Petiole reach to 3.0 cm. long. Blade 2-3 pinnatisect, deltoid-ovate in outline with ovate-dentate segments and hairy. Flowers in compound inflorescence. Umbels terminal and antiphyllous, peduncled, 3-7 rayed. Rays unequal 1.0-2.5 cm. long. Bract and bracteoles present, numerous and conspicuous. Bract simple, entire or trisect, linear to lanceolate with acute to acuminate apex, hairy, 5.0 - 10.0 x 1.0 - 2.0 mm. Bracteoles simple, linear-oblong, hairy, 4.0 - 6.0 x 1.0 mm. long. Flower small, bisexual and perfect. Calyx teeth distinct. Petal white, obovate to oblong with notched inflexed apex, 1.0 -1.5 x 0.1 mm. Stamens reach to 1.0 mm long. Ovary sub-inferior with conical stylopodium and short style. Fruit ellipsoid, 6-10 mm long, hairy, compressed with spiny margins and brownish. Primary mericarp ribs five filiform, hairy, while secondary ribs four with 2 - 3 rows of glochidate spines.
