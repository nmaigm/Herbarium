Eruca sativa Mill.
Gard. Dict., ed. 8,no.1 (1768).

Synonyms:
Eruca lativalvis Boiss., Fl. Orient. 1:396 (1867).

Morphology:
Annual herb, up to 60 cm. long, glabrous or sparingly hairy. Stem erect, terete, solid, simple-branched at the base. Internodes 1.5 – 6.5 cm. x o.2 - 0.3 cm. Leaves lobed and long. Lower leaves obovate in outline and petiolate. Petiole glabrous and up to 4 cm. long. Blade 4.5 -8 cm. x 1.5 – 2.5 cm, lyrate- pinnatisect with 2-3 laterals ovate to oblong, entire, acute lobes. Terminal lobe obovate with dentate-serrate margin and obtuse apex. The upper leaves small with very short petiole. Petiole up to 0.4 cm. long. Blade ovate in outline with 2 lateral ovate, dentate, acute lobes, 2.3 – 4.3 cm. x 0.6 – 1.2 cm. Inflorescence raceme. Flowers 12 - 19 mm. long and pedicellate. Pedicels glabrous, 1 – 3 mm reach to 5 mm. long in fruit. Sepals green, glabrous, 7 - 9 mm. x1- 1.5 mm., ovate-oblong in outline with narrow membranous margin. Petals creame to yellow with dark violet veins 12 - 19 mm. x 1 – 2 mm. clawed, limb obovate with obtuse apex. Stamen 5 – 12 mm. long, with glabrous filament and oblong anthers. Ovary smooth with 10 – 12 mm. long style and capitate stigma. Siliqua 2 – 3.5 cm. x 0.3- o.4 cm., erect, glabrous, oblong, dehiscent with distinct midrib. Beak 0.5 – 1 cm. long, flat and seedless. Seeds 1.5 – 2.5 mm. x 1.2- 2 mm. sub-globose, brownish yellow with terminal hilum and reticulate- rough surface.
