Amaranthus albus L.
Syst. Nat., ed. 10, 2: 1268 (1759).

Morphology:
Annual herb 20-60 cm; stems erect or procumbent, much-branched; leaves 2-3 x 2-6 cm, oblong or spathulate; margin undulate; apex obtuse to slightly emarginate, mucronate, base cuneate; inflorescence of short, axillary cymose clusters; bracteoles twice as long as the perianth-segments, with spinose apex; perianth-segments 3, narrowly elliptic; capsule 1.5-1.75 mm, dehiscing transversely, slightly muricate.
