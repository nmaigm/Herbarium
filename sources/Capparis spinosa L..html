Capparis spinosa L.var. spinosa
Sp. Pl., ed. 1, 503 (1753).

Synonyms:
Capparis aegyptia Lam., Encycl. 1: 605 (1785).
Capparis spinosa L. var. aegyptia (Lam.) Boiss., Fl. Orient. 1: 420 (1867).

Morphology:
Shrub 30-80 cm, stems trailing or ascending; leaves 1-4.5 x 0.5-4 cm, mostly ovate-orbicular; flowers solitary on pedicels 1.5-5 cm; sepals 1-2 cm, subequal; petals 1-2.5 x 0.5-1.5 cm, subequal, white, obovate; stamens 2-3.5 cm, numerous; gynophore 2-4 cm; fruit 3-6 cm, pyriform; seeds numerous, Four varieties occur in Egypt Plant glabrous; stipules often spinose; filaments of stamens pink; fruit 2.5-5.5 x 2.5-4 cm. O. D, S; cliffs, rocky slopes, desert wadis.
