Deverra tortuosa (Desf.) DC
Prodr.4:143 (1830).

Synonyms:
Bubon tortuosum Desf., FI.Atlant. 1:257, t.73 (1798).
Pituranthos tortuosus (Desf.) Benth.ex Asch. & Schweinf., Mem. Inst. Egypt.2:80 (1887).

Morphology:
Perennial aromatic green, glabrous shrub up to 80 cm. high. Stem erect, striate, with dichotomously divaricatly branched. Internodes terete and long up to 10.0 cm. Leaves caducous, lower leaves. 2.5 â€“ 6.0 cm, 2-pinnatisect with linear & acute lobes and ovate in outline. Sheath oblong-linear, acute, 1.0 -1.5 cm. long, with broad scarious margin. Petiole short, 0.5 -1.0 cm. long. The upper leaves reduced to sheaths with acute apex and reach to 1.7x 0.5 cm. Flowers in compound umbel inflorescence. Umbels with long peduncle 1.3 - 3.5 cm., terminal with 6-9 rays. Rays unequal 0.5 -1.5 cm. long. Bract and bracteoles present. Bract long-triangular or long ovate 2.0 - 2.5 x 1.0mm. with acuminate apex and scarious margin. Bracteoles oblong with sinuate-scarious margin, 1.3 -1.5 x 1.0 mm. Flower pedicelate, bisexual and perfect. Calyx teeth minute-obsolete. Corolla greenish-white. Petal ovate notched with long inflexed apex and hairy on the vein at the back. Stamen 5 with equal dorsifixed anther lobes. Ovary broad ovoid-sub-globose with long style and depressed stylopodium. Fruit, globose or ovoid, 1.5 mm. long, laterally compressed, hairy, mericarps with 5 prominent ribs.
