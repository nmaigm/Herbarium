Chenopodium glaucum L.
Sp. Pl., ed. 1. 220 (1753).

Morphology:
Annual herb 10-40 cm, glaucous, glabrous except on the lower surface of leaves; stems erect, ascending or procumbent, branched at the base, angular, striate; leaves 1-5 x 0.3-1 cm, elliptic, lanceolate or ovate, green and glabrous on the upper surface, glaucous and farinose on the lower, the margins sinuate-serrate; basal and median leaves petiolate, the uppermost almost sessile; inflorescence of terminal rminal and axillary spike-like cymes; perianth-segments 2-4, connate at the base, the margins broadly membranous; stamens 2-3; pericarp thin, loose; seeds 0.8-1 mm, vertical, occasionally horizontal in lateral flowers, reddish-brown,Â glossy.
