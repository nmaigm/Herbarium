Anethum graveolens L.
L., S P. ed. 263 (1753); Boiss., F I. 2: 1026(1872).

Morphology:
Annual green and erect aromatic, glabrous herb up to 65 cm. long. Stems much branched from above. Internode terete reach to 0.9 cm. long. Leaves alternate, pinnatisect and reach to 15.0 cm. long. Lower leaves, petiolated, sheathing at the base. Sheath long obovate, 2-2.5 x 0.5-0.6 cm. with membranous margins. Petiole 2-3.5 cm. Blade 5.0-9.0 cm, ovate-oblong in outline, 3-4 pinnatisect with linear or filiform segments. The upper leaves smaller and sessile 3.5 - 4.0 cm. long. Sheath 1.3–1.5 cm x 0.3 - 0.4 cm. Blade 2.2 - 2.5 x 0.3 - 0.5 cm. triangular in outline and 2-3 pinnatisect. Flowers in compound umbel, umbel terminal with long peduncle up to 12.0 cm. and many rayed; 15-25 rayed, rays equal 1.3-3.0 cm. long. Bract and bracteoles absent. Flower pedicelate, hermaphrodite and perfect. Calyx teeth obsolete. Corolla yellow, 5 petals oblong, uninerved, notched with inflexed apex. Stamen with 1.0 mm. long filament and dorsifixed equal anther lobes. Ovary with short erect styles which deflexed in fruiting. Stylopodium conical and longer than the styles. Fruit elliptical, glabrous, dark brown, laterally compressed, 3.0 - 6.0 mm. long. Carpophore filiform, bipartite to the base, mericarps with 5 filiform prominent ribs (the marginal ribs are ± winged).
