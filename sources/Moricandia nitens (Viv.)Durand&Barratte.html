Moricandia nitens (Viv.)
Durand & Barratte, Fl. Libyc.Prodr.15(1910).

Synonyms:
Hesperis nitens Viv.,Fl.Libyc.Spec.38,t.5,f.3(1824).
Moricandia dumosa Boiss., Diagn. Pl. Orient. ser. 1,8:25 (1849).

Morphology:
Perennial, dwarf shrub, glabrous, up to 50 cm. long. Stem erect, terete, solid and much branched. Internodes 2 – 3.7 cm. x o.1 - 0.2 cm. Leaves up to 5 cm. long. Lower leaves obovate in outline with short petiole. Petiole up to 0.5 cm. long. Blade 2 – 4.5 cm. x 5.5- 10 cm., simple with undulate margin and acute apex. Upper leaves sessile, elliptic, entire and acute apex. Inflorescence racemes. Flowers 9 – 13 mm. long, and pedicellate. Pedicels glabrous, 1 – 3 mm long. Sepals yellowish-green, glabrous, 6 – 8 mm. x 1 – 2 mm., oblong-obovate in outline with narrow membranous margin. Petals pale violet with dark nerves 8 - 13 mm. x 2.5 mm., clawed, limb obovate with obtuse apex. Stamen 6 - 9 mm. long, with glabrous filament and oblong anthers. Ovary smooth with 1 – 2 mm. long, glabrous style and long (decurrent) stigma. Siliqua 2 – 3.5 cm. x 0.1 - 0.2 cm., erect, dehiscent, narrowly linear- tetragonous, valves with distinct midrib. Beak short;3mm. long, seedless. Seeds in one row. Seeds 1 – 1.5 mm x o.5 – 0.8 mm., oblong-obovoid with sub terminal hilum, smooth surface and brown colour.
