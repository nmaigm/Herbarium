Ridolfia segetum (L.) Moris
Enum. Sem. Hort. Taur. 1841:43 (1841).

Synonyms:
Anethum segetum L., Mant. Alt. 219 (1771).

Morphology:
Annual erect glabrous herb 30-80 cm long. Stems dichotomously branched with terete and striated Internode. Internode reach to 11.0 cm long and 0.5 cm width. Leaves, alternate, triangular-ovate to ovate, deeply pinnatisect into filiform lobes. Lower leaves petiolated and long, 9.o - 20 cm. Petiole 1.5 - 4.0 cm. long with basal sheath. Sheath long -triangular 1.5 - 2.0 x 0.6 -1.0 cm with membranous margin. Upper leaves sessile, smaller with short sheaths, 0.8 -1.5 x 0.2 – 0.3 cm with narrow membranous margin. Flowers in compound umbels. Umbels with long peduncle, 2.0 – 6.5 cm, mostly terminal with many rayed 17–22 rays. Rays slender, unequal, 2.5 – 4.5 cm. long. Bract and bracteoles absent. Flower pedicelate, pentamerous, tetracyclic and bisexual. Pedicels short, 2.0- 5.0 mm. long. Calyx teeth absent. yellow, oblong, 1.0 x 0.5mm, uninerved and retuse to emarginated-truncate with inflexed apex. Stamens 5 with long filament, 1.0-1.5 mm and equal, dorsifixed anthers. Ovary of syncarpous, 2 locule, sub-inferior, stylopodium depressed and discoid shorter than the patent-recurved styles. Fruit 1.5-2.0 X1.0-1.2 mm, laterally compressed, oblong-cylindrical to elliptic, glabrous and dark greenish-brown. Carpophore bipartite; mericarps with five inconspicuous ribs.
