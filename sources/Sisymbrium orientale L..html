Sisymbrium orientale L.
Cent. PI. 2:24, no.173 (1756).

Morphology:
Annual, pubescent, shortly retrose-hispid herb, up to 75 cm. long. Stem erect, terete, solid and branched. Internodes 1.5 -3.3 cm. x o.1 - 0.3 cm. Leaves long and obovate-oblong in outline. Lower leaves petiolate and reach to 19 cm. long. Petiole hairy and up to 5.5 cm. long. Blade 5 -12.5 cm. x 4 - 6 cm., usually lyrate-pinnatipartite with triangular ovate, obtuse, repand-dentate to crenate and acute terminal lobe and 3 - 5 pairs of lateral lobes. The upper leaves are simple and petiolate. Petiole up to 4 cm. long. Blade hastate, entire, triangular-ovate in outline, 2.2 - 8.5 cm. Inflorescence are racemes. Flowers bisexual, actinomorphic, hypogynous, yellow 3 - 6 mm. long and pedicellate. Pedicels hairy 2 - 5 mm. long and thick. Calyx, four sepals. Sepals greenish, 3 -4 mm. x 0.5 – o.1 mm., oblong-obovate in outline with few hairs. Corolla, four petals. Petals yellow or whitish-yellow, 4.5 -7 mm. x 2 - 4 mm., clawed, limb obovate with obtuse apex. Androecium, six stamens, tetradynamous. Stamen 4 – 7 mm. long with glabrous filament and oblong anthers. Gynoecium with two carpels, parietal placentation. Ovary hairy with glabrous, short style, 1-3 mm. long and bi-lobed stigma. Siliqua 5 – 1o cm. x o.1- o.2 cm., hirsute rarely glabrous, erect with distinct mid-vein. Valves thick walled 3- nerved. Seeds in one row, indehiscent, with thick septum and short beak, 1-2 mm. long. Seeds minute, 1 – 1.5 mm. x 0.5 – 1 mm., oblong, sub-terminal hilum, reticulate-smooth surface and orange brown colour.
