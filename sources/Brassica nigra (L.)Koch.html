Brassica nigra (L.)
Koch in RÖhling, Deutschl. Fl., ed. 3, 4:713(1833).

Synonyms:
Sinapis nigra L., Sp. Pl., ed. 1, 668(1753).
Brassica bracteolata Fisch. & C. A. Mey., Index Sem. Hort. Petrop. 4:33(1838).

Morphology:
Annual herb, glabrous. Stem erect, terete with ribbed, solid and branched above the base. Internodes 3-5.5 cm. x 0.2-0.3 cm. Leaves up to 24 cm. long. Lower leaves obovate- oblong in outline and petiolate. Petiole up to 5 cm. long. Blade 8- 18.6 cm. x 5.5- 8 cm., lyrate-pinnatisect, obovate, serrate-dentate margin, obtuse apex, with 2-6, oblong-ovate lateral lobes. Upper leaves simple with short petiole. Petiole up to 2 cm. long. Blade asymmetric 2.3 - 5 cm. x 0.8 - 2 cm., elliptic, entire with acute apex. Inflorescence racemes. Flowers 3 - 6 mm. long, and pedicellate. Pedicels glabrous, up to 10 mm. long. Sepals yellowish-green, glabrous, 3 – 4.5 mm. x 0.9 – 1.1 mm., long ovate in outline with narrow membranous margin. Petals yellow 3 - 6 mm. x 1.5 – 2 mm., clawed, limb obovate with obtuse apices. Stamen 3 - 4 mm. long, with glabrous filament and oblong-ovate anthers. Ovary smooth with 1 – 2 mm. long, glabrous style and capitate stigma. Siliqua 1.5 - 3 cm. x 0.5 – 1 mm., cylindrical, torulose, appressed to stem, dehiscent and slightly curved. Beak narrow, 2.5mm. long, seedless. Seeds in one row. Seeds globose; 1- 1.5 mm x 1 – 1.5 mm., brown with reticulate surface and terminal hilum.
