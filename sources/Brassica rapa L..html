Brassica rapa L.
Sp. Pl., ed. 1,666 (1753).

Synonyms:
Brassica campestris L., Sp. Pl., ed. 1, 666(1753).

Morphology:
Annual or biennial, glabrous herb, up to 80 cm. long. Stem erect, terete, solid and branched at the base. Internodes 2– 3.5 cm. x o.1 - 0.2 cm.. Leaves up to 12 cm. long. Lower leaves obovate in outline and petiolate. Petiole glabrous and up to 5 cm. long. Blade 4 -7 cm. x 2.5 - 5 cm., lyrate with lateral crenate to dentate lobes and acute apex. The upper leaves sessile, lanceolate in out line with aurical base and entire margin 2.8 – 5 cm. x 0.6 – 1.2 cm. Inflorescence raceme. Flowers 6 - 10 mm. long and pedicellate. Pedicels glabrous, up to 21 mm. Sepals yellow- green, glabrous, 4 - 6 mm. x1- 2 mm., obovate-oblong in outline with narrow membranous margin. Petals yellow 6 - 8 mm. x 3 – 5 mm., clawed, limb obovate with obtuse apex. Stamen 4 – 7 mm. long, with glabrous, broad filament and oblong anthers. Ovary smooth with 3 – 4 mm. long style and capitate stigma. Siliqua 4 – 8 cm. long x 0.2- o.3 cm. width, ±terete, one-veined on each valve, dehiscent and glabrous. Beak 1 – 1.5 cm. long, one seeded or seedless. Seeds 1 – 1.5 mm. long x 1-1.3 mm. width, ovoid-sub globose with terminal hilum, smooth surface and brownish colour.
