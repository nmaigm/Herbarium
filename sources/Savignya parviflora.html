Savignya parviflora (Delile) Webb
Giorn. Bot. Ital. 2(2): 215 (1847).

Synonyms:
Lunaria parviflora Delile, Descr. Egypt, Hist. Nat. 248 (1814). Savignya aegyptiaca DC., Syst. Nat. 2: 283 (1821), nom. illeg.

Morphology:
Annual glabrous herb 10-40 cm; stems erect, branched from the buse, lower leaves 25x1-2.5 cm, oblong-elliptic to obovate, sinuate-dentate, petiolate: upper leaves linear-entire, calyx 2-3 mm, petals 3.5-5 mm; fruiting pedicels 1-25 cm, capillary, spreading or deflexed; silicula 1-14 x 0.5-0.8 cm, strongly compressed, shortly stipitate valves yellow-green, septum membranous; style 1-2 min; stigina 2-lobed, seeds 0.8-1 mm, discoid, broadly winged
