Matthiola incana (L.).
R.Br. in Aiton, Hort. Kew, ed. 2,4:120(1812).

Synonyms:
Cheiranthus incanus L., Sp. Pl.662(1753).

Morphology:
Perennial herb, woody at the base up to 80 cm. long. Stem erect, angular, solid and branching from the base. Internodes 1 – 2.3 cm. x o.2 - 0.4 cm. Leaves simple with short petiole. Petiole up to 1.2 cm. long. Blade 7.5 – 12 cm. x 0.9 – 2 cm., elliptic-lanceolate with entire margin and acuminate apex. Inflorescence racemes. Flowers 12 - 18 mm. long and pedicellate. Pedicels hairy 4 – 7 cm. reach to 10 cm. long in fruit. Sepals 7 – 10 mm. x 2 –3 mm., green to violet, oblong-ovate in outline with narrow membranous margin and densed hairs. Petals bright yellow, 11 - 18 mm. x 4 – 10 mm., clawed, limb obtriangular, wavy margin and obtuse apex. Stamen 6 – 14 mm. long with glabrous filament and oblong anthers. Ovary hairy with 2 – 4 mm. long, hairy style and bi-lobed stigma. Siliqua 3 – 7 cm. x 0.2 – 0.3 cm., linear, torulose, dehiscent, glabrous with short straight horns. Seeds in one row. Seeds 2.3 – 3 mm. x 1-1.5 mm., broad-ovate with sub-terminal hilum, rough surface and brown colour.
