Amaranthus viridis L.
Sp. Pl., ed. 2, 1405 (1763).

Synonyms:
Amaranthus gracilis Poir. in Lam., Encycl. Suppl. 1: 312 (1810).
Albersia caudata (Jacq.) Boiss., Fl. Orient. 4: 992 (1879).

Morphology:
Annual herb 15-75 cm; stems erect to ascending, angular; leaves 1.5-6.5 x 1.5-3 cm, deltoid-ovate to ovate-rhombic; the apex obtuse or slightly emarginate; petiole to 8 cm; flowers in axillary cymose clusters, forming a paniculate terminal inflorescence; bracteoles shorter than the perianth-segments, ovate, acuminate; perianth-segments 3, 1-1.75 mm, ovate-oblong; capsule 1-1.5 mm, subglobose, irregularly dehiscing or indehiscent, strongly muricate.
