Morettia canescens Boiss.
Diagn. Pl. Orient., ser. 1, 2(8): 17 (1849).

Morphology:
Perennial herb, grey-canescent, appressed stellate-hairy; stems 20-50 cm, much branched, prostrate; leaves 1-1.5 cm, almost sessile, oblong-ovate to elliptic, entire or often dentate, acute, the margins revolute; sepals 3.5-4.5 mm; petals 5-9 mm, white or pink; siliqua 0.8-1.5 x 0.1-0.2 cm, straight or slightly curved, appressed to the stem, torulose; stigma with 2 divergent lobes, on a slender style.
