Brassica tournefortii Gouan .
Observ. Bot. 44, t. 20 A (1773).

Morphology:
Annual herb, up to 80 cm. long, glabrous above, hispid below. Stem erect, terete, solid and spreading branched. Internodes1.1 – 2.9 cm. x 0.1 – 0.3 cm. Leaves lyrate-pinnatisect up to 30 cm. long, hispid, especially on midrib. Lower leaves oblong-obovate in outline and petiolate. Petiole up to 4.5 cm. long. Blade, 10.5 – 26.5 cm. x 4.5 – 5 cm., lyrate-pinnatisect with 6-12 Paris of lateral lobes. Lobes oblong, serrate-dentate margin and obtuse to acute apex. Upper leaves simple with short petiole. Petiole up to 0.7 cm. long. Blade 3 – 7 cm. x 1 – 1.3 cm., oblong –ovate with entire or denticulate margin and acute apex. Inflorescence racemes. Flowers 3 -7 mm. long, and pedicellate. Pedicels hairy, up to 16 mm. long. Sepals green to green violet, hairy, 3- 4 mm. x 0.5 - 1 mm., long-ovate in outline with narrow membranous margin. Petals whitish-pale yellow 5 - 7 mm. x 0.5 – 1 mm., clawed, limb obovate with obtuse apex. Stamen 3 – 6 mm. long, with glabrous filament and sagitate oblong anthers. Ovary smooth with 1 – 1.5 mm. long style and capitate stigma. Siliqua 3.5 – 5.6 cm. x 0.05 - 0.2 cm., torulose, slightly curved, dehiscent, glabrous. Beak 1- 1.6 cm. long with 1-2 seed. Seeds globose, 1- 1.5 mm. x 1 - 1.5 mm. with terminal hilum, reticulate surface and dark brown colour.
