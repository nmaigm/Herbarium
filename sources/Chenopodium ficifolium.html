Chenopodium ficifolium Sm.
Fl. Brit. 1: 276 (1800).

Morphology:
Annual herb 25-80 cm, whitish-green, mealy; stems erect or ascending, branched, angled, striate; leaves 2-10 x 0.5-3 cm, distinctly longer than wide, mealy beneath, some 3-lobed with lateral lobes triangular and shorter than the median, the apex obtuse, the margins coarsely toothed; inflorescence of terminal and lateral paniculate cymes, mealy. dense at first, becoming lax in fruit; perianth-segments 5, connate for 1/2 of their length, densely farinose, keeled towards the tip, the margins membranous; stamens usually 5; pericarp firmly adherent to the seed; seeds 1.2 mm, horizontal, keeled, black, glossy.
