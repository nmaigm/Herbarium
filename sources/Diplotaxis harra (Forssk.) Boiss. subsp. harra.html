Diplotaxis harra (Forssk.) subsp. harra
Boiss., Fl. Orient. 1:388 (1867).

loubu
Sinapis harra Forssk., Fl. Aegypt.- Arab. 118(1775).

Morphology:
Perennial herb, up to 60 cm. long, hispid rarely glabrous. Stem often woody, erect, terete, solid and branched at the base. Internodes 1 – 2.5 cm. x o.2 - 0.4 cm. Lower leaves simple and petiolate. Petiole up to 3.5 cm. long. Blade oblong obovate in outline, tapering to petiole, dentate with acute apex, 4.3 – 5.3 cm. x 2 – 3 cm. Upper leaves petiolate. Petiole up to 1.9 cm. long. Blade elliptic ovate in outline with dentate margin and acute apex, 3 – 4 cm. x 2 – 2.5 cm. Inflorescence corymb. Flowers 3 - 7 mm. long and pedicellate. Pedicels glabrous, 3 – 10 mm. long. Sepals greenish, hairy, 3 -6 mm. x 0.8 - 1mm., oblong-long obovate in outline with narrow membranous margin. Petals yellow 4 -7 mm. x 1 – 2.5 mm., clawed, limb obovate gradually tapering to claw with obtuse apex. Stamen 3.2 – 4.5 mm. long, with glabrous filament and sagitate anthers. Ovary smooth with 3 – 4 mm. long style and bi-lobed stigma. Siliqua 1.7 – 3 cm. x 0.1- o.2 cm., torulose, deflexed and glabrous; dehiscent with membranous septum and short beak, 0.8 -1mm. long. Seeds in two rows. Seeds oblong-ellipsoid, 0.4 – 1 mm. x 0.3 – 0.5 mm., with sub terminal hilum, glandular surface and pale brown colour.
