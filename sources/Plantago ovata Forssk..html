Plantago ovata Forssk.
Fl. Aegypt.-Arab. 31 (1775).

Synonyms:
Plantago decumbens Forssk., Fl. Aegypt.-Arab. 30 (1775).

Morphology:
terete; spikes Em stemless; leaves (1.5-)5-8(-25) x (0.1-)0.3-1 cm, alternate, in rosettes, from broad Eglandular grey to white-hairy, scapose or short-stemmed annual, rarely perennial, 1-15 clasping base, linear to lanceolate, acute, entire or with a few small teeth; peduncles 1-10-30), (0.5-12-12 cm, erect or ascending, shorter to long shorter to longer than leaves, terete; (0.5-)1-2(-5) x 0.8-1(-1.5) cm, ovoid, very dense; bracts acts 1.5-4 x 1.5-3 mm, the lower broadly ovate, the upper orbicular to obovate, broadly scarious-margined; sepals 2.35 mm, elliptical to obovate, flat to cymbiform, broadly scarious-margined; anterior free of connate at base: posterior subequal, or one flat and placed behind the flower; corolla mostly glabrous, the lobes 2-3 x 2-2.5 mm, apiculate; capsule 2-seeded; seeds 2-2.5 mm thick-walled cymbiform.
