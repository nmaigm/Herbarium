Enarthrocarpus strangulatus Boiss.
Diagn. Pl. Orient. 8: 44 (1849).

Morphology:
Annual herb 10-50 cm, densely hispid-hirsute; stems procumbent or ascending, branched from the base; basal leaves 4-12 cm, lyrate-pinnatisect; lateral lobes dentate; cauline leaves subsessile, pinnatifid or dentate; inflorescence dense, the bracteate throughout sepals 4-6 mm, hispid; petals 1-1.5 cm, cream or yellow, purple-veined, clawed; pedicels 0.8-1.2 cm, curved; siliqua 2.8-4.5 cm, slightly compressed, strongly longitudinally striate; the lower segment 3-4 mm, 1- to 2-seeded the upper segment 8-to ressed, strongly torulose, hispid, 12-seeded, separating at maturity into 1-seeded segments; beak 4-5 mm, conical-flattened; seeds 1 mm, brownish.
