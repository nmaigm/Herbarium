Heliotropium curassavicum L.
Sp. Pl ., ed. 1, 130 (1753).

Synonyms:
Heliotropium glaucum Salisb., Prodr. 113 (1796).
Heliotropium glaucophyllum Moench, Math. Suppl. 147 (1802).
Heliotropium chenopodioides Willd., Enum. Pl. Hort. Berol. 175 (1809).

Morphology:
Glabrous halophytic perennial, 30-80 cm; stems terete, much-branched from the base, turning greyish-black when dry; leaves 1-5 x 0.2-1 cm, narrowly spathulate to oblong-linear, entire, the apex obtuse to acute, the base attenuate; petiole 1-5 mm; inflorescence terminal, simple or forked, spike-like; cymes dense, elongate, lax in fruit; flowers sessile, ebracteate; calyx 1-2 mm, divided almost to base, not accrescent in fruit; corolla 1.5-2.5 mm, white, urceolate, glabrous outside; tube 1-1.5 mm; lobes 0.5-0.75 mm, broadly ovate, the middle nerve terminating below the rounded apex; stamens inserted Â± at the middle of the corolla-tube; anthers 0.75 mm, ovate, shortly apiculate; ovary 0.5 mm; style 0; stigma 0.5 mm, appressed-conical, glabrous; fruit subglobose, of 4 nutlets; nutlet 1.5-2 mm, ovoid, sulcate, brown, glabrous.
