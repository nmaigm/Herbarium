Citrullus colocynthis (L.) Schrad.
Linnaea 12: 414 (1838).

Morphology:
Scabrous perennial; stems 0.5-1.5 m. procumbent, branched, angular, hirsute; root fleshy; leaves rough on both surfaces; leaf-blade 3-10 x 2-5 cm, triangular-ovate, deeply 5-7 lobed; lobes lobate, sinuate-undulate, the base cordate; petiole 1-3 cm, rigid; tendrils simple, short; male flowers: on pedicels 1-2 cm; receptacle-tube 1.5-2.5 mm, broadly obconical; calyx-lobes 2-4 mm, lanceolate; petals 1 x 0.5 cm, yellow, connate at the base: female flowers: on pedicels 1-4.5 cm; ovary 8 x 6-8 mm, subglobose, hairy; fruit 6-12 cm diam., globose, smooth, green, mottled with yellow or entirely yellow when ripe, with a firm wall, fleshy, on a stalk 1.5-5.5 cm; seeds 6-9 x 3.5-4 mm, ovate, compressed.
