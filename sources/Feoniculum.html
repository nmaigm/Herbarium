Foeniculum vulgare Mill
Gard. Dict., ed.8, no.1 (1768).

Synonyms:
Anethum foeniculum L., Sp. Pl., ed.1, 263(1753).
Foeniculum officinale All., Fl. Pedem.2:25 (1785).

Morphology:
Perennial green and erect aromatic herb, basally woody and up to 180 cm tall. Internode sulcate, terete, reach to 10 cm long. Leaves alternate, ovate, 2-4 pinnatatisect with filiform linear segments. Lower leaves petiolate and reach to 17 cm long. Petiole with conspicuous ribbed basal sheath. Sheath long triangular-ovate; 1.5 - 4.0 X 0.2 - 0.5 cm with membranous margin. Upper leaves sessile, few and much reduced, sometimes represented by basal sheath with a short aristate appendage. Flowers in compound umbels inflorescence. Umbel with long peduncle; 7.0–14.0 cm long, terminal and axillary with many rayed; 15-25 rays. Rays unequal; 2.0-4.5 cm long and glabrous. Flowers pedicellate, ebracteate, pentamerous, tetracyclic, bisexual and actinomorphic. Pedicel short; to 0.8 mm long. Calyx with five very minute sepals and calyx teeth absent. Corolla valvate, petal yellow ovate; 1.0X1.0 mm, uninerved and notched-emarginate with inflexed apex. Androecium 5 stamens, stamen distinct; 1.0-1.5 mm long, anther lobes are equal, dorsifixed and yellow. Gynoecium of two carpels, syncarpous. Ovary sub-inferior with short and recurved styles. Stylopodium conical. Fruit is acremocarp, oblong ovoid to cylindrical 4.o – 6.0 X 2.0 - 3.0 mm scarcely compressed, glabrous and brownish. Carpophore bipartite, mericarp with five prominent stout ribs.
