Pulicaria undulata (L.) C. A. Mey.
Verz. Pfl. Casp. Meer. 79 (1831) subsp. undulata

Synonyms:
Inula undulata L., Mant. 115 (1767).
Aster crispus Forssk., Fl. Aegypt.-Arab. 150 (1775).
Inula crispa (Forssk.) Pers., Syn. Pl. 2: 450 (1807).
Francoeuria crispa (Forssk. ) Cass., Dict. Sci. Nat. 34: 44 (1825).
Pulicaria crispa (Forssk.) Oliv. in Grant, Trans. Linn. Soc. London 29: 96 (1873).
Francoeuria undulata (L.) Lack in Rech. f., Fl. Iranica 145: 120 (1980).

Morphology:
Low shrub, often cushion-shaped, or perennial with woody base, 30-80 cm; stems numerous; juvenile branches densely white-appressed-woolly, later glabrescent; old branches reddish and divaricate; leaves 0.5-3(-4) x 0.2-0.8(-1.2) cm, oblong-linear, oblong-lanceolate or oblong, sessile, clasping at the base, the apex mucronate, the margin conspicuously undulate, first white-cobwebby, later glabrescent; leaves on flowering branches usually very small; capitula solitary, numerous in lax terminal cymes; involucre 4-5 mm, pubescent; phyllaries 5-6-seriate, acuminate or cuspidate, ciliolate, with minute sessile glands; florets 2.5-3.5 mm, the ray florets ± as long as the disc florets; corollas yellow; achenes 0.5-1.2 x 0.35 mm, ± cylindrical, glabrous; pappus 2-2.5 mm, of 7-10 inner barbellate bristles, connate with an outer cupule at the base.
