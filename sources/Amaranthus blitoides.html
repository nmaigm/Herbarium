Amaranthus blitoides S. Watson
Proc. Amer. Acad. Arts 12: 273 (1877).

Morphology:
Annual herb 10-50 cm; stems prostrate or decumbent, much-branched, glabrous, densely leafy; leaves 0.4-1 x 0.1-0.5 mm, white-nerved on the lower surface, obovate to linear-lanceolate, obtuse; the margins membranous; petiole 2-4 mm; inflorescence of short, axillary cymose clusters, leafy to apex; bracteoles shorter than the perianth, lanceolate; perianth-segments 3-5, unequal, similar to the bracteoles, the longest 2-2.5 mm; capsule subglobose, dehiscing transversely; seeds glossy, black or reddish, reticulate.
