Enarthrocarpus lyratus (Forssk.).
DC., Syst. Nat. 2:661(1821).

Synonyms:
Raphanus lyratus Forssk., Fl. Aegypt.-Arab. 119(1775).

Morphology:
Annual, hispid with scattered simple hairs, up to 45 cm. long. Stem ascending to erect, terete, solid and branched from the base. Internodes 1.5 – 2.8 cm. x o.2 - 0.3 cm. Leaves up to 19 cm. long. Lower leaves obovate - oblong in outline and petiolate. Petiole up to 2.3 cm. long. Blade 5.6 – 16.5 cm. x 5.5- 10 cm., lyrate-pinnatisect, with 4-5 oblong, dentate and acute lateral lobes. Terminal lobes obovate in outline with dentate and acute apex. Upper leaves elliptic and petiolate. Petiole up to 1.2 cm. long. Blade 1.4 – 4.5 cm. x 0.8- 2 cm., elliptic, entire and acute. Inflorescence racemes. Flowers yellow 14 - 19 mm. long, and pedicellate. Pedicels with few hairs, 5 – 8 mm. Sepals greenish, glabrous, 6 – 10 mm. x 1 – 1.5 mm. width, oblong-elliptic in outline with narrow membranous margin. Petals white with violet nerves 14 - 19 mm. long x 1.5 – 2 mm. width, clawed, limb obovate with obtuse apices. Stamen 7 - 11 mm. long, with glabrous filament and oblong-ovate anthers. Ovary smooth with 3 – 4 mm. long, glabrous style and capitate stigma. Siliqua 2.8 – 5 cm. x 0.1 - 0.3 cm., compressed, indehiscent, sometimes slightly curved and segmented.
