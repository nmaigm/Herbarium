Eryngium glomeratum Lam.
Encycl. 4:755 (1798).

Morphology:
The morphological characters are similar to those in E. campestre differs only with regard to: 1-Plant glaucous, up to 80 cm. long.
2-Stems, many, sparingly branched and strongly ribbed.
3-Basal leaves shorter, reach to 15 cm. long.
4-Petiole with long conspicuous-nerved leaf sheaths.
5-Blade 2 - 3 pinnatisect.
6-Bract subulate, entire, mostly 5, 3.0 - 4.0 cm. long.
7-Fruit flattened covered with scales, crowned with the persistent calyx teeth.
