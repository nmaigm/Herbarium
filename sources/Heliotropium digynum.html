Heliotropium digynum (Forssk.) Asch. ex C. Chr.
Dansk Bot. Ark. 4 (3): 14 (1922).

Synonyms:
Lithospermum digynum Forssk., Fl. Aegypt.-Arab. 40 (1775).
Heliotropium luteum Poir. in Lam., Encycl. Suppl. 3: 22 (1813), nom. illeg.

Morphology:
Perennial with a woody base, 20-50 cm; stems erect or ascending, terete, canescent: leaves 1-2 x 0.5-1 cm, ± ovate, the margin undulate, the apex obtuse to acute, shortly cuneate at the base; petiole 2-5 mm; inflorescence terminal, spike-like, simple or forked: cymes lax in fruit; flowers ebracteate, subsessile; calyx 2-3 mm, persistent, lobed almost to base, the lobes equal, tomentose; corolla 3.5-5.5 mm, yellow, urceolate-tubular, silky outside, glabrous inside; lobes 0.8-1.5 mm, acute, inflexed; anthers 1-1.2 mm, oblong, sessile; ovary 0.5-0.8 mm, glabrous; style 1.2-1.5 mm; stigma 0.8-1 mm, ± conical or obscurely 2-lobed, hairy; fruit ovoid, usually of 4 nutlets; nutlet 2.5-4 mm, brown, silky.
