Coronopus didymus (L.) Sm.
,Fl.Brit. 691 (1800).

Synonyms:
Lepidium didymum Desf., FI.Atlant. 1:257, t.73 (1798).
Senebiera didyma (L.) Pers.,Syn. Pl. 2:185 (1806).

Morphology:
Annual, puberulous, herb, up to 40 cm. long. Stem prostrate to ascending, terete, solid and much branched. Internodes 1 – 2 cm. x o.1 - 0.2 cm. Leaves up to 8 cm. long. Lower leaves forming a rosette, oblong-obovate in outline and petiolate. Petiole up to 2.2 cm. long. Blade 2.4 – 5.6 cm. x 5.5- 10 cm., pinnatisect with ovate-elliptic lobes. Upper leaves oblong ovate, small with short petiole. Petiole up to 0.6 cm. long. Blade, pinnatisect with ovate-elliptic lobes. Inflorescence racemes. Flowers minute 1 – 2 mm. long, with long pedicel. Pedicels hairy, 1 – 3 mm long. Sepals yellowish-green, glabrous, 0.5 – 1 mm. x 0.4 mm., obovate in outline with scarious margin. Petals white, shorter than sepals 0.3 – o.5 mm. x o.2 – o.3 mm. Stamen 1 – 1.5 mm. long, with glabrous filament and globose anthers. Ovary reniform, smooth with 1 – 1.5 mm. long, glabrous style and capitate stigma. Silicula 1.5 – 2 mm. x 2 – 2.5 mm., subreniform, reticulate,indehiscent, deeply emarginate with narrow septum, one seeds per locule. Beak 0.5 mm long. Seeds 1- 1.5 mm x 0.5 - 0.8 mm., kidney shape with terminal hilum, smooth surface and brown colour.
